With more emphasis on students’ academic success, this research applies machine learning models like linear regression, ridge regression, random forest and XGBoost on personal, family and school factors of 6590 students aiming to find important predictors of exam scores and provide guidance to facilitate student’s academic outcome. Linear regression model yields the highest R squared at the value of 0.77, suggesting a linear relationship between the independent variables and the target. The results emphasized that while higher exam scores are mostly determined by the students themselves, the effect of family and school should not be ignored. More personalized assistances should be provided to students to address potential obstacles and boost performance.
